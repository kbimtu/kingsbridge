Kingsbridge Institute is a non-profit organisation to help anyone (and especially young people) be prepared for the challenges of the 21st century, especially in equipping them with the skillsets and mindsets to be competitive in the labour market and to develop innovations.

We are dedicated to raising awareness of STEM beyond the students with a propensity for STEM, so students can make informed choices in their academic and professional careers. We encourage and support practical, legal and compliant STEM projects.

We work with government, academia, and industry to promote STEM literacy through education, research, and other activities and services. We also work with the general public, industry and subject matter experts, regulators and policymakers to shape the future of STEM education.

The name of this non-profit organisation is inspired by a historically notable problem in mathematics, the Seven Bridges of Koenigsberg ("Königsberger Brückenproblem"). Euler's solution to the problem using graph theory showcased the power of mathematical reasoning and abstraction in understanding real-world phenomena.

The Kingsbridge Institute aims to serve all those who will be affected by the emergence of emerging technologies in the near and far future.

To best serve the needs of youths, activities are directed at the following groups of students: primary schoolers (ISCED 1), junior secondary schoolers (ISCED 2), senior secondary schoolers (ISCED 3), and undergraduate tertiary students (ISCED 4-6), including recent graduates.

To best serve the needs of others, we work with local, regional, and global partners to address relevant beneficiaries, especially the vulnerable and marginalized groups.

The Kingsbridge Institute divides the type of its activities by targeting of different beneficiaries, i.e. primary-, secondary-, tertiary-level, and post-tertiary-learners.

For each type of beneficiary, there are different emphases on the same topics or learning directions. Learning is experienced through training, workshops, coaching, advisory, self-paced explorations, and competitions.

The focus of the Kingsbridge Institute for this decade (2020s) is on the data-driven digital society (AI, machine learning, data sciences, data analytics, data privacy, and generally, big data) and trust-enabled digital society (blockchain, cryptography, and generally, web3 and various applications such as DeFi, NFT, and metaverse where appropriate).

Almost all activities are hosted at an academic institution. For example, a secondary school may host a programme for its students and occasionally invite those from other schools to attend. Another usual arrangement is a university hosting the activity and opening it for all schoolers and students in the region.

Activities are usually held after school and on Saturdays. Occasionally, activities that are part of the curriculum would be held during school hours. Activities of an international nature would be multi-day affairs that may constitute additional travel arrangements. 

The guiding vision of the Kingsbridge Institute is to encourage and equip the next generation of innovative and inquisitive minds to direct their creativity and passion for designing solutions for the sustainable development of humanity.

The mission of the Kingsbridge Institute for this decade (2020s) is to educate and advocate on artificial intelligence, privacy-enhancing technologies, trust-enabling technologies from first principles to practical, sustainable solutions for young people and other vulnerable and marginalized communities.
